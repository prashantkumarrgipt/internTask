Background
Current practice of general dietary recommendations are conflicting & highly subjective depending upon source bias & does not take into account how each food impacts various biochemical activities taking place inside the body. This results in the occurrence of multiple chronic health conditions, often expressed by your body as symptoms indicating that something wrong is going inside the body. We acknowledge the fact that our body does not have enzymes to digest 65% of the food we eat & ultimately everything rests on 40 trillion microbes living in our gut to metabolize these foods & release beneficial nutrients & compounds to help our body grow & thrive. These microbes living in our gut perform critical functions such as digesting food, regulating essential hormones, boosting metabolism, modulating satiety level, providing nutrients- vitamins, minerals & beneficial short chain fatty acids, detoxifying enzymes, neutralizing pathogens & making neurotransmitters. The generic dietary recommendations have led to mismatch between chemicals in the food we eat & digestive enzymes released by our gut which has caused dysregulations of multiple biological functions inside the body. This is the root cause of why we are experiencing multiple chronic health conditions today.



There is strong epidemiological & molecular evidence that what we eat & how our microbes metabolize the food we eat contributes significantly to onset & progression of various chronic diseases. Generic dietary advice which has become the hallmark of various plain vanilla disease management & nutrition companies is obscure & conflicting which has done more harm than good. Scientific Publications often contradict one another with one suggesting health benefits while another propagating harmful impact foriii the same food or diet. Consumption of dairy consumption has contradictory views with some studies evidence supporting association with cancer & chronic diseases while others showing no correlation & some showing significant health benefits. Similarly recent studies published contradictory views on the effect of saturated fats on cardiovascular diseases. Red meat is said to provide essential nutrients such as Vitamin B12 & dietary protein yet there is strong evidence in favor of a correlation between consumption of these foods & increased incidence of inflammatory diseases & cancer. The fact is current nutritional guidelines are influenced by the food industry which promotes increased consumption of sugar, dairy & meat.



This large ecosystem of conflicting nutritional information has led to the creation of hundreds of diets, each one claiming health benefits. This leaves consumers confused & frustrated with no confidence in results & suggestions. Nutrition science finds itself in a predicament for a number of reasons.



This branch of science does not take into account the role of gut microbiome in personalizing diet in spite of a number of evidence proving the fact that impact is significant. Besides, researchers make mistakes when they categorize food as good & bad for everyone without understanding that the same food can have different impacts on different people. Now it has been proved that our genetics plays a minor role & our gut microbiome has a major role to play in our nutrition choices. Our gut microbiome not only impact how food is digested but also converts molecular compounds in foods into beneficial or harmful secondary metabolites that have larger impact on human physiological functions such as production of neurotransmitters, release of inflammation (immune system activation & deactivation), immune tolerance & carbohydrate metabolism. Each food can be viewed as a container of molecular ingredients( micronutrients) rather than macronutrients( homogeneous material) which is either good or bad for our health. Besides, metabolic functions of each individual’s unique microbiome need to be identified & quantified in order to prescribe specific foods that contain molecular, chemicals & substrates that will be converted into beneficial secondary metabolites such as short chain fatty acids(SCFA) by an individuals’ unique microbiome. Besides microbial functions also need to be quantified to ascertain which food should be avoided which have molecular compounds that will be converted into harmful secondary metabolites such as lipopolysaccharides(LPS) by an individual’s gut microbiome. These insights coupled with biological testing of customers,expert knowledge, clinical studies & customer feedback powered by an intelligent data analysis model can be then applied to each individual’s unique microbiome to recommend best foods to be consumed & foods to be minimized & avoided.
